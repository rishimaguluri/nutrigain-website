Origin & Design Process

How We Got Here

From a simple frustration with campus dining to a validated product concept — here's how Nutrigain was born through rigorous user research and iterative design.

Where It Began

A Real Problem We Lived Every Day

Every one of us experienced the same pain: heading to a dining hall between classes, not knowing if it's packed, not knowing what's nutritious, and spending precious minutes hunting through Nutrislice's clunky interface.

As OSU students, we saw firsthand how the tools available failed the 60,000+ students on campus. The data was there — buried. The crowding info didn't exist. The schedule integration was nonexistent.

We decided to fix it — by building the tool we always wished existed.

Design Process

How We Got to Nutrigain

A rigorous, user-centered design process from discovery to prototype.

🔍
Step 01 — Discovery

User Research & Problem Identification

We conducted interviews with OSU students to understand their dining frustrations. Key insight: students knew what to eat but couldn't easily access the information to make healthy choices in the time they had.

6+ Interviews Observation Sessions Survey Data
🗣️
Step 02 — Synthesis

User Needs Ranking & Prioritization

Using pairwise comparison analysis, we ranked 7 user needs from high to low importance. Easy nutrition access topped the list, followed by easy access to dining hall information, and alleviating decision fatigue.

1 Easy access to nutrition information
2 Easy access to dining hall information
3 Alleviating decision fatigue / mental load
💡
Step 03 — Ideation

Concept Brainstorming

We brainstormed 6 distinct concepts ranging from a gamified nutrition app to physical dining hall kiosks to a QR code scanner system. Each was sketched and evaluated against user needs.

Concept 1: OSU App Integration
Concept 2: University Chatbot
Concept 3: Nutrition Quest (not selected)
Concept 4: Kiosk System (not selected)
📋
Step 04 — Selection

Concept Screening & Scoring

We used a formal screening matrix (+ / 0 / –) and a weighted scoring matrix to evaluate all concepts. The OSU App Integration scored 8.45 and the Campus Chatbot scored 8.25 — both selected for development.

OSU App Integration 8.45 / 10 ✓ Selected
Campus Chatbot 8.25 / 10 ✓ Selected
📐
Step 05 — Design

Detailed Design & Requirements

We created formal, testable requirements derived from validated user needs. 4 High Priority and 4 Medium Priority requirements defined the scope of the OSU App Integration module.

🚀
Step 06 — Prototype

Prototype Plan & Sprint 1

Built with Python + Streamlit, FastAPI, PostgreSQL, Redis, and Azure. Sprint 1 focuses on the AI meal scanner, chatbot, and dietary profile sync — directly addressing the top 3 user needs.

Python + Streamlit FastAPI GPT-4o-mini

Our Decision

Six Concepts, Two Winners

After rigorous evaluation, two concepts rose above the rest.

CONCEPT 01

University App Integration

Integrated module inside of existing university apps with nutrition tracking, real-time crowding alerts, habit dashboard, and AI meal scan.

✓ Selected
CONCEPT 02

University Chatbot

Conversational AI layer built on top of Concept 1 — answers natural language dining queries, proactive alerts, and hall FAQs.

✓ Selected

Concepts Not Moving Forward:

Concept 3: Nutrition Quest (Score: 54) | Concept 4: Kiosk System (Score: 56) | Concept 5: QR Scanner (Score: 61) | Concept 6: Standalone App (Score: 71)
